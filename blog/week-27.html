Week 27: Designing the Writable Kitchen State & Meal Plan Schemas

As VITA approaches a stable release, our software team has focused on a major transition: moving from read-only data lookups to full read-write kitchen state management. A private coach must know not only what is in the global database, but what is currently in your fridge and what equipment is resting on your kitchen counter.

To support this, we designed the schemas for libinventory and updated the user database structures in user_profile.db. By keeping all user inventory and meal plans separate from the static recipes catalog, we ensure that user configurations are never wiped out when a new global catalog database updates.

The segregated database architecture of VITA: static recipe database catalogs are read-only and replaced wholesale on app updates, while fridge inventory and optimized meal plans live in the user profile database.

Under this schema, librecipe acts as a read-only Binary Guard Filter. When the user queries a recipe, this layer automatically filters outcomes based on allergen constraints, dietary requirements, and preparation times before presenting candidates. Simultaneously, libinventory manages fridge items (deducting quantities FIFO by expiration date) and tracks equipment statuses (owned, temporary, or functional), feeding clean inputs directly to the optimizer.
