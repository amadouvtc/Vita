Week 5: Testing Whisper STT on Raspberry Pi 4

The core user interface of VITA is voice. We want the user to simply say: "I just ate a bowl of yogurt with honey," and have it instantly logged. This requires an accurate, offline Speech-to-Text (STT) model. We chose OpenAI's Whisper model because of its state-of-the-art accuracy.

We compiled whisper.cpp on the Raspberry Pi 4 to utilize hardware-specific optimizations. We loaded the Whisper Tiny model (the smallest model with decent accuracy) and ran our first transcription tests. The results were disheartening.

Benchmark chart comparing transcription latency of Whisper Tiny on various edge platforms. The Orange Pi 5 NPU achieves near-instantaneous execution compared to CPU runs.

A simple 3-second audio recording took over 15 seconds to transcribe. The Pi's CPU was pinned at 100%, and the temperature spiked immediately. A 15-second delay is completely unacceptable for a seamless user experience. We tried quantizing the model to 4-bit integers, which brought it down to 8 seconds, but it was still too slow and degraded accuracy. We must look for faster hardware chips.
